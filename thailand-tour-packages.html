Complete Thailand Travel Guide: Questions & Answers

Best Time to Visit Thailand

When is the best time to visit Thailand?

The best time to visit Thailand is from November to February (cool season). December-January offers pleasant weather (25-32°C), low humidity, and minimal rainfall, perfect for beach activities and sightseeing. March-May is hot season (28-38°C) with higher humidity. June-October is monsoon season with heavy rainfall, especially on west coast (Phuket, Andaman). East coast (Koh Samui, Gulf islands) is drier during monsoon. Songkran (April) is water festival - book in advance.

What is Thailand's weather like?

Thailand has tropical climate with three seasons: Cool Season (Nov-Feb): 25-32°C, low humidity, pleasant. Hot Season (Mar-May): 28-38°C, humid, pre-monsoon showers. Rainy Season (Jun-Oct): 25-35°C, heavy afternoon downpours, typhoons possible. Bangkok is hot year-round. Northern Thailand (Chiang Mai) is cooler in winter (can be 10°C). Southern islands have different patterns based on monsoons.

Is Thailand good during monsoon?

Monsoon (Jun-Oct) offers fewer tourists and 20-30% discounts. However, west coast beaches (Phuket, Krabi) experience heavy rain and rough seas. East coast (Koh Samui, Koh Tao) is drier and good during monsoon. If traveling during rainy season, focus on Bangkok, Chiang Mai, or east coast islands. Rain usually comes in short, heavy bursts. Carry umbrella/raincoat.

Food & Cuisine

What is famous food in Thailand?

Must-try dishes: Pad Thai (stir-fried noodles), Tom Yum Goong (spicy shrimp soup), Green Curry (Gaeng Keow Wan), Massaman Curry, Som Tam (papaya salad), Mango Sticky Rice, Tom Kha Gai (chicken coconut soup), Pad Kra Pao (basil stir-fry), Satay (grilled skewers), Boat Noodles, and fresh coconut. Street food culture is amazing. Try at local markets and street stalls. Spice levels: request "not spicy" if needed.

Where to eat in Thailand?

Eat at street food stalls and local markets for best value. Recommendations: Bangkok: Khao San Road, Chinatown (Yaowarat), Or Tor Kor Market, Sukhumvit Soi 38. Pattaya: Floating market, Beach Road. Phuket: Old Town, Bangla Street. Chiang Mai: Night Bazaar, Warorot Market. Avoid overpriced tourist restaurants near beaches. Food costs: ₹50-200 at street stalls, ₹300-800 at restaurants. Street food is safe and delicious.

Is Thailand vegetarian-friendly?

Thailand is moderately vegetarian-friendly. Buddhist influence means many vegetarian options. Order "Jay" (vegetarian) or "Mangaswirk" (no meat). Options: Pad Thai Jay, Som Tam (no fish sauce), coconut curry with vegetables, rice dishes, fresh fruits, and mango sticky rice. Vegetarian restaurants exist in Bangkok and tourist areas. Street vendors may not understand - visit dedicated veg restaurants. Learn to say "no meat, no fish."

Weather & Climate

What is Thailand's climate like?

Thailand has tropical climate with three seasons: Cool/Dry (Nov-Feb): 25-32°C, low humidity, comfortable. Hot (Mar-May): 28-38°C, humid, pre-monsoon showers. Rainy (Jun-Oct): 25-35°C, heavy afternoon downpours. Bangkok is hot and humid year-round. Northern Thailand (Chiang Mai, Chiang Rai) is cooler (10-20°C in winter). Southern islands have monsoons varying by coast.

When is monsoon season in Thailand?

Monsoon (rainy season) is May-October, peaking September-October. West Coast/Andaman (Phuket, Krabi): Heavy rain Jun-Oct, rough seas. East Coast/Gulf (Koh Samui, Koh Tao): Rainy Oct-Dec, rough seas Dec-Feb. Bangkok receives short afternoon showers year-round. Best beach time: West Coast: Nov-Mar; East Coast: Mar-Oct. Always check forecasts, especially for island trips.

Safety & Travel Tips

Is Thailand safe for solo travelers?

Yes, Thailand is generally safe for solo travelers, including females. Thai people are friendly. Take standard precautions: avoid overpriced tuk-tuk rides (use Grab), beware of scams (Gem scam, tuk-tuk tours), keep belongings secure, and avoid accepting drinks from strangers. Popular solo spots: Bangkok (nightlife, culture), Chiang Mai (yoga, trekking), islands (beaches). Respect local customs and dress modestly at temples.

Is Thailand safe for families?

Yes, Thailand is very family-friendly. Resorts offer kids' clubs and activities. Beaches are calm in certain areas. Popular family spots: Phuket (beaches), Pattaya (water parks), Chiang Mai (cultural experiences), Bangkok (temples, shopping). Use private transport for convenience. Be cautious with street food hygiene for kids. Medical facilities in tourist areas are good. Carry medications. Respect local culture (especially at temples).

What scams should I avoid in Thailand?

Common scams: Overpriced tuk-tuk rides (use meter or Grab), closed temple scam (tuk-tuk says temple closed, takes to gem shop), jet ski rental scams (damage charges), fake police, ping pong show scams, Gem scam (fake gemstones), and restaurant overcharging. Always confirm prices, use reputable operators, avoid unsolicited offers, and keep valuables secure. Use hotel safes. Bargain at markets but not at malls/official shops.
